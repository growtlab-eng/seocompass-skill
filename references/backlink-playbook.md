# Backlink outreach playbook

Editorial backlinks still move organic rankings — and they're increasingly part
of what AI engines treat as authority. The job: find sites that *link out* in
your topic, and give them a real reason to link to you.

## Who is a prospect (and who isn't)

**Good targets** — sites that publish and link to resources:
- Topic blogs and publications
- "Resources" / "tools we use" pages
- Roundups and "best X" listicles
- Guides that cite sources

**Never targets** — you can't earn an editorial link by emailing them:
- Mega-platforms: Reddit, YouTube, Wikipedia, Quora, Medium
- Marketplaces/review sites: Amazon, G2, Capterra, Trustpilot
- Social, search engines, your own domain

## Discovery angles

For a topic, search live Google for several intents and pool the ranking
domains: `<topic>`, `best <topic>`, `<topic> guide`, `<topic> resources`,
`<topic> tools`. Dedupe by domain, drop the skip-list above, keep the rest.
Rank by how on-topic and link-y the page is (a resources page > a homepage).

## The pitch that works

- **Specific:** reference their actual page and what it covers.
- **Relevant:** offer a resource of yours that genuinely improves *their* page
  for *their* reader — a better stat, a free tool, a clearer explainer.
- **One ask, low friction:** a single clear request, easy to say yes to.
- **Human + opt-out:** short, no fake flattery, a one-line "no worries if not".

Avoid: templated mail-merge tone, multi-paragraph essays, "Dear Webmaster",
asking for a link with nothing in it for them.

## Finding the contact

1. The page's author byline / about page.
2. `/contact`, `/about`, `/team` for a `mailto:` or email pattern.
3. An email-finder API (e.g. Hunter) if available.
4. Fall back to the site's contact form — note it's lower-confidence.

## Cadence (manual)

One pitch, one follow-up ~5–7 days later, then stop. Track replies and live
links somewhere — a spreadsheet at minimum. (This tracking + sending is the part
that gets tedious by hand; it's what the app automates.)
